People

People are everybody who appears on the programme in some form. They have the following attributes:

  • Name - their real name. This is broken into three separate fields, labelled as First name, Middle name and Last name, but really you can use them however seems best - as long as at least one of these three fields is filled in, Streampunk will be happy. The division is mainly to allow different sort orders.
  • Badge - their badge name. This is optional, but if specified, this is what'll be used everywhere in public listings, instead of Name.
  • Memnum - membership number. As assigned by your membership database, or -1 if not known. This isn't updated automatically, btw, so if you create a record for someone who has joined the con, look them up in the membership database first. If they haven't joined yet, put -1 in. Later, you'll have to go back and update it with their actual membership number, after they've joined.
  • Email - their email address. This just should just the email address, so:
    • fred@site.com is okay
    • Fred Smith <fred@site.com> is wrong
    • fred@site.com (Fred Smith) is wrong
  • Contact details - whatever means the Programme staff will use for communicating with this person.
  • Public Notes - most likely bio information about guests. This data will be visible in the public programme.
  • Gender - You can have any number of different values here, not just Male/Female. Or you can ignore it entirely. It's here so that you can use it to get a feel of how gender-balanced your programme is.
  • Dist email - you should set this to Yes if the person has said they're happy for the con to distribute their email address to other programme participants, for purposes of pre-con discussions.
  • Recording Okay - Set this to Yes if the person has confirmed they're happy to be recorded, for any and all programme items they might be on.
  • Complete - use this to indicate that you've got all the information you need from this person, and that you're not expecting to make any more changes to them.
  • Tags - Each person can have an arbitrary number of tags associated with them.
  • Availability - indicates when they're available for scheduling on programme items.

At time of writing, all of this information is entirely separate from the membership database; Streampunk can't pull this information out of Richard's system. Plus, it'll often be the case that you know someone will be doing something on the programme, but they haven't joined yet, and we have to cater for that.

Listing People

You can list people by:

  1. Going to the "People" page via the link on the banner, and following the "List" link

You get a list of people, sorted alphabetically by Last Name. Options for sorting differently will be available later.

The list includes:
  • The person's name, as a link. Clicking on the link takes you to the person's record.
  • A checkbox, with a label that says either "Select?" or "Email/Select?".

Beneath the list there are four buttons, for emailing all or some people, and for saving all or some people. The "Some" buttons only apply to those peope you've checked. The "Email" buttons only apply to those people who actually have an email address.

If you click on the "Email" buttons, you get a form where you can fill in a subject and message, which will be sent to each of the people on the list. There are choices to include, for each person:

  • A list of any programme items they're scheduled for
  • Their contact details
  • When Streampunk believes they are available

The "Save" buttons allow you to save this list of people, with a label, and later come back to it. Most likely, you'll do this for lists of people that come from checks and reports, but not always.

Clicking on a person's name in the list shows you their details, plus:

  • All the programme items this person is associated with
  • All the tags (if any) associated with this person

There are also a link that'll take you to the page where you can edit a person's details, and a link for adding more tags to the person.

Creating a new person

You can create new people by:

  1. Going to the "People" page via the link on the banner, and following the "Create" link

Modifying a person's details

The "Items for person x" page is accessible from the People:List links, or most places where you see a person's name. This page, in addition to listing all their items, has the link to "Edit person."

Just click on the link, modify the details how you'd like, and click on Submit. The details get updated, and you back to the Person page again.
